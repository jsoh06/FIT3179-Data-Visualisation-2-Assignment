LRT and Train Lines in Malaysia

LRT Station Distribution

This map displays the distribution of LRT stations across the Klang Valley.

The station distribution reveals the system's emphasis on serving both the dense urban core around Kuala Lumpur and the sprawling suburban developments that characterize the Klang Valley.

Ridership Over Time

Ridership is the number of trips, not the number of unique individuals/passengers.

The LRT Kelana Jaya Line consistently maintains the highest ridership throughout the period, while KTM Intercity remains the lowest with steady usages. Most lines show a clear recovery pattern from mid-2021 onwards as restrictions eased and people returned to work after the COVID-19 pandemic.

Weekday v.s Weekend

Only the top 4 train lines with the most ridership are displayed here.

LRT Kelana Jaya's weekday ridership consistently exceeded weekend usage by significant margins throughout the years. Weekday ridership is generally 60-80% higher than weekend ridership for LRT and MRT, reflecting their role in serving daily commuters. Certain months showed reduced ridership during holiday periods, while the consistent weekend patterns suggest stable recreational usage regardless of season.

Route Comparison

LRT Kelana Jaya routes offer shorter journey times (2-60 minutes) with moderate fares (MYR 1.1-4.0). MRT Kajang routes show longer journeys (10-81 minutes) with higher fares (MYR 2.1-6.4). Multi-line routes requiring transfers fall in the middle range (8-64 minutes, MYR 1.4-5.7). The MRT Kajang line offers better value for extended trips, while LRT Kelana Jaya provides the most cost-efficient options for shorter urban journeys.
