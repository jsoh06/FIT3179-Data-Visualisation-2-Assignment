LRT and Train Lines in Malaysia

Malaysia's rail transport network in the Klang Valley has developed significantly since the 1990s to address growing urban congestion. The Light Rail Transit (LRT) system forms the core of public transportation, with two main lines serving different corridors. The LRT Kelana Jaya Line runs from Gombak in the north to Putra Heights in the south, while the LRT Ampang Line connects Sentul Timur to Ampang in the east. These lines intersect at several interchange stations, allowing passengers to transfer between routes.

This map displays the distribution of LRT stations across the Klang Valley, showing how the two main lines serve different corridors of the metropolitan area.

The station distribution reveals the system's emphasis on serving both the dense urban core around Kuala Lumpur and the sprawling suburban developments that characterize the Klang Valley, with notable clusters of stations in major residential and commercial areas like Petaling Jaya and Puchong.

Ridership Over Time

Ridership is the number of trips, not the number of unique individuals/passengers.

The LRT Kelana Jaya Line consistently maintains the highest ridership throughout the period, reaching over 240,000 daily passengers by 2024, while KTM Intercity remains the lowest with steady usage around 15,000-20,000 passengers. Most lines show a clear recovery pattern from mid-2021 onwards as restrictions eased and people returned to work, though the recovery rates vary significantly between different rail services. This reveals how urban rail lines like LRT and MRT were more severely affected by lockdowns compared to intercity services, reflecting the different travel patterns and purposes of their passenger bases.
