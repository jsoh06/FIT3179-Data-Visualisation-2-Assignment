LRT and Train Lines in Malaysia

The station distribution reveals the system's emphasis on serving both the dense urban core around Kuala Lumpur and the sprawling suburban developments that characterize the Klang Valley, with notable clusters of stations in major residential and commercial areas like Petaling Jaya and Puchong.

Ridership Over Time

Ridership is the number of trips, not the number of unique individuals/passengers.

The LRT Kelana Jaya Line consistently maintains the highest ridership throughout the period, reaching over 240,000 daily passengers by 2024, while KTM Intercity remains the lowest with steady usage around 15,000-20,000 passengers. Most lines show a clear recovery pattern from mid-2021 onwards as restrictions eased and people returned to work after the COVID-19 pandemic, though the recovery rates vary significantly between different rail services.

Weekday v.s Weekend

Only the top 4 train lines with the most ridership are displayed here.

LRT Kelana Jaya shows the most dramatic difference, with weekday ridership consistently exceeding weekend usage by significant margins throughout the year. The data shows that weekday ridership is generally 60-80% higher than weekend ridership for urban lines like LRT and MRT, reflecting their role in serving daily commuters.
